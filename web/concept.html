What is it?

A game for the XO-PC. The game uses fueling a rocket to get it into space to teach fractions, specifically arithmetic operations. Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division are covered, as well as decimal to fraction conversion and proportions.

What Does it Use?

The game was created to run on the Sugar Operating System on the XO-PC. It is built in Python with PyGame.

Interface:

  • Player starts at start screen, then they can continue progress or select any unlocked lesson (stage) and the difficulty
  • Lesson shows goal containers, choice containers overlain on the rocket art.
  • Each choice container has a fraction and the player must choose the correct fraction(s) to match the goal container's.
  • Click to select, click again to deselect to transfer liquid to big container
  • Must correctly use ratios and other fraction operations to fill container correctly
  • A Reset Button allows the player to reset the lesson. A Check Answer button is used. If the answer the right, the rocket is fueled and goes higher. Otherwise, the player has to try again.

Level System:

  • Different "lessons" are taught for each "journey" or trip from the ground to space
  • As the rocket gets higher, the concepts get more advanced/in-depth
  • Each lesson has a learning goal, which is met when the final problems are solved and the rocket is in space
  • At the end of a lesson, a cool graphic is displayed
